Linus Torwalds

Creator of Linux

Biography

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish software engineer and creator of the Linux operating system. He was born in Helsinki in 1969 and studied at the University of Helsinki. In 1991, he released the first version of Linux, which has since become one of the most widely used open-source operating systems in the world. Torvalds continues to oversee the development of Linux and is also involved in various other software projects.

Achievements

  • 1991: Linus Torvalds creates the first version of the Linux operating system and releases it under the GNU General Public License.
  • 1994: Linux version 1.0 is released.
  • 1996: Torvalds receives the EFF Pioneer Award from the Electronic Frontier Foundation.
  • 1999: Time magazine names Torvalds one of the 100 most influential people of the 20th century.
  • 2000: Torvalds joins the Open Source Development Labs (now known as the Linux Foundation) as a fellow.
  • 2003: The first Linux Kernel Summit is held, bringing together developers and contributors to discuss the future of Linux.
  • 2005: Torvalds receives the Lovelace Medal from the British Computer Society.
  • 2012: Torvalds receives the Millennium Technology Prize, a prestigious award given every two years to groundbreaking technological innovations.
  • 2015: The Linux kernel reaches version 4.0.
  • 2018: Torvalds takes a brief hiatus from overseeing the Linux kernel development due to controversy surrounding his management style, but returns after apologizing and committing to making changes.
